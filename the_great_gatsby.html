Description

"The Great Gatsby," written by F. Scott Fitzgerald and published in 1925, is a classic American novel that delves into the excesses and illusions of the Jazz Age during the 1920s. Set against the backdrop of Long Island, New York, the story follows the enigmatic millionaire Jay Gatsby and his unrequited love for Daisy Buchanan

Narrated by Nick Carraway, a newcomer to the area, the novel provides a glimpse into the lives of the wealthy elite and their hedonistic pursuits. Gatsby's extravagant parties, his obsession with Daisy, and his mysterious past create an aura of mystery and allure. Through vivid prose and symbolic imagery, Fitzgerald explores themes of wealth, social class, love, and the elusive American Dream.
